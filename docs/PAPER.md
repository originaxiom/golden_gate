# golden_gate: an exact-arithmetic toolkit for Fibonacci-anyon braid gates

**DRAFT — a tools paper, not a novel-algorithm paper.** This document describes the `golden_gate`
software and its design choices. It makes **no claim to new braid-synthesis algorithms** (the
synthesis literature is mature and cited in §6); its contribution is clean, exact, tested, installable
packaging and integration, plus a research core and its quantum-topology connection. Every empirical
figure below is reproduced by the test suite and is tagged with its epistemic label —
**[exact]** / **[numeric]** / **[ported]** (see `GOVERNANCE.md` §3).

## Abstract

`golden_gate` is a Python library for computing with Fibonacci anyons: it turns quantum gates into
anyon braids and braids into gates, and it backs the theory's headline identities with **exact
arithmetic in the cyclotomic field ℚ(ζ₆₀)** rather than floating point. Identities such as `F² = I`,
the Yang–Baxter relation, and `Jones(4₁; e^{2πi/5}) = 1 − √5` are therefore proved as symbolic
equalities and locked by equality tests, not verified to a `10⁻¹⁴` tolerance. The library is built
"inside-out": a tested core of exact/high-precision engines, a pre-registration + banked-identity
research harness on top, and an outward-facing braid compiler and interactive explorer. We describe
the architecture, the exactness discipline, a consolidated high-precision research core (an E₆
cup-product deformation study), and the reproducibility apparatus.

## 1. Introduction

Topological quantum computation proposes to store and process quantum information in the braiding of
non-abelian anyons, where the computation's robustness is a topological invariant rather than a matter
of error rates. Fibonacci anyons are the canonical universal example. Working with them numerically is
routine; working with them *exactly* — so that a claimed identity is a theorem of the arithmetic and
not a small residual — is less common in tooling. `golden_gate` is organized around that distinction.

The software also serves a second purpose: it consolidates a set of exact and high-precision
computational engines that had accreted, in a scattered form, inside a long research program (the
origin-axiom probes). Packaging those engines cleanly — with real imports, scoped precision, banked
identities, and tests — is itself a contribution, independent of any single result they compute.

## 2. Background

A Fibonacci anyon `τ` has the fusion rule `τ×τ = 1+τ` and quantum dimension `d_τ = φ = (1+√5)/2`
**[exact]**. On the qubit encoded by three `τ`'s of total charge `τ`, braiding is generated by two
`2×2` unitaries `σ₁, σ₂` built from the R-matrix `diag(e^{-4πi/5}, e^{3πi/5})` and the F-matrix
`[[1/φ, 1/√φ],[1/√φ, −1/φ]]`. The R-phases are 60th roots of unity; `1/√φ` is not cyclotomic, so the
F-matrix identities are handled symbolically. The **golden gate** is the figure-eight braid
`σ₁⁻¹σ₂σ₁⁻¹σ₂` — a non-Clifford **[numeric]** rotation of `0.2447π` **[numeric]** — which, with the
Clifford gates, is universal.

## 3. Architecture

The library has three layers:

1. **Core engines** (`golden_gate.core`). `cyclo` implements exact ℚ(ζ₆₀) arithmetic as length-16
   `Fraction` vectors modulo `Φ₆₀`, with the radical constants `√5, √−3, √−15`, the Gauss sum
   `g(15)=i√15`, and the level-15 Weil `T`/`S` matrices. `charvar` is a theta-lift / seam toolkit on
   top of it. `lie`/`jets` are the high-precision research core (§5). All precision is scoped through
   `core.precision`; no engine mutates the global working precision at import.
2. **Gate-attack harness** (`core.harness`). A `Preregistration` freezes the hypothesis, nulls, kill
   conditions, and banked identities before any compute; `run_gated` runs the banked-identity gate
   first and **refuses to read a result past a failed gate**.
3. **Demo layer** (`golden_gate.demo`) and the **interactive explorer** (`web/explorer.html`). The
   outward face: braiding matrices, named gates, exact-backed knot/Jones data, the compiler, and
   text/SVG braid diagrams; the explorer reimplements the same math in the browser (float64) with an
   explicit honesty banner.

## 4. Exactness as the design differentiator

A floating-point toolkit verifies `F² = I` as `‖F² − I‖ < 10⁻¹⁴`. `golden_gate` instead computes the
cyclotomic data exactly and locks the identity with an equality assertion. Concretely, the figure-eight
Jones value at the Fibonacci point is returned as an exact field element and tested
`jones_at_fibonacci("figure_eight") == 1 − √5` **[exact]**. This buys two things: identities are proved,
not approximated; and *convention* errors are forced into the open — the standard value `1 − √5` and
the unnormalized Kauffman-bracket value `−φ` are both documented, related by the exact identity
`−φ = (1 − √5)·φ²/2` **[exact]** (`GOVERNANCE.md` §2 makes this convention honesty a binding rule).

The braid *compiler* itself is a conventional brute-force / structured search over the generators; it
uses float64 for speed and is **not** presented as novel (§6). The novelty of the package is exactness
and integration, not synthesis.

## 5. The research core (ported; firewalled)

`core.lie` and `core.jets` are a consolidated, high-precision study of the E₆ character variety of the
figure-eight — **[ported]** research machinery in Lie theory and low-dimensional topology, with an
explicit firewall: *no physics claim* is made. In one line each: `e6` is the exact Chevalley `𝔢₆` with
the Jacobi identity verified over all 76,076 basis triples **[exact]**; `rep` computes the tangent
`H¹(4₁, Sym^{2m})` and its ℤ/2 gradings; `cohomology` computes the second-order cup-product obstruction
`[z∪z] ∈ H²(π₁(4₁), 𝔢₆)` and finds the θ-odd "escape" directions `m∈{4,8}` **unobstructed to second
order** **[ported]**; `jets` extends this to third order (Massey) and computes the boundary restriction.
The verdict is stated at its true scope: "unobstructed to 2nd/3rd order" is the Goldman–Millson reading
of a vanishing cup class, not a claim of all-orders integrability. The harness (`demo_e6`) runs this as
a gated, pre-registered campaign.

## 6. Related work

Exact and near-optimal synthesis of Fibonacci-anyon braids is an established literature:
Kliuchnikov, Bocharov and Svore, *Asymptotically optimal topological quantum compiling*; Kliuchnikov
and Yard, *A framework for exact synthesis* (arXiv:1504.04350), which gives exact synthesis over ℤ[φ]
via maximal orders; and Monte-Carlo compilers (e.g. PRX Quantum 2.010334). `golden_gate` does **not**
claim to advance this frontier. Its compiler is a straightforward search; the package's contribution is
orthogonal: exact-arithmetic backing, a tested and installable consolidation, the research core, and
the outward-facing explorer. (`GOVERNANCE.md` MB5 requires this prior-art search before any novelty
claim, of which we make none.)

## 7. Reproducibility

Every figure in this paper is a test. The fast tier (`pytest -q`, ~70 s) runs the exact locks and
structural checks; `OA_SLOW=1 pytest -q` adds the heavy exact/high-precision sweeps (symbolic
Yang–Baxter, the full theta-lift, the banked E₆ obstruction verdicts). `python -m golden_gate.core.gates`
runs stdlib-only banked-identity and hygiene gates and exits non-zero on any regression. The library was
subjected to two independent multi-agent adversarial audits (port faithfulness, test rigor, honesty,
numerical robustness, and a refutation pass against the banked verdicts); the findings and fixes are in
`PROGRESS_LOG.md`.

## 8. Limitations and firewall

The explorer computes in float64 (~`10⁻¹²`); it is a convenience, and the library — not the page — is
where the exact proofs live. The research core is exact/numeric mathematics, **not** a physics result;
nothing here bears on a "theory of everything." The compiler is not competitive with the cited
synthesis algorithms and is not intended to be. The package name collides with unrelated software
(a PyPI `golden-gates`, an IoT stack, a bio assembly method); a published distribution would need a
distinct name.

## 9. Availability

`originaxiom/golden_gate`, MIT-licensed. `pip install -e .`; the interactive explorer is
`web/explorer.html` (self-contained, no build). The mathematics is in `docs/MATH.md`, the API in
`docs/API.md`, worked examples in `docs/EXAMPLES.md`.
